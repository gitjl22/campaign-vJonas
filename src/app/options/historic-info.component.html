Election of 1916 background:

The election of 1916 was a close race, in which Woodrow Wilson won reelection.

Central issues of the election included: Entering World War 1, Segregation in the South, and Women's Right to Vote.

Electoral Vote distribution and state prefrences were very different in 1916 than they are today.

Alaska and Hawaii were not states yet in 1916! They became states in 1959.

Washington DC had no electoral votes in 1916, the 23rd ammendment was passed in 1961 to give them the power to vote for president.
